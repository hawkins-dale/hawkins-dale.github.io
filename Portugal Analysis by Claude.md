## [[Porto]] 
**⭐ Top Pick**

This is your Torino equivalent — a real, working city that happens to be beautiful. It has a thriving local economy beyond tourism, a fantastic metro and bus system, and incredible walkability (though it’s hilly).

## Évora
**🏛️ Underrated Gem**

A small, UNESCO-listed city in the Alentejo region, with almost no mass tourism.

One hour 20 minutes on the train from Lisbon

Roman temple, a bone chapel, medieval walls — history with genuinely dark and interesting stories

Exceptional Alentejo cuisine (arguably Portugal’s best regional food)

Small enough to walk everywhere

Downside: it’s best as a 2–3 day stop, not a week-long base, and public transport connections are decent but not as strong as Porto/Lisbon

## Lisbon
**With Caveats**

Lisbon is magnificent but has moved closer to the “too touristy” end in recent years, especially Alfama and Baixa. However, it’s so good that I’d still recommend it with a strategy:

Stay in Mouraria, Intendente, or Arroios — authentic neighborhoods where locals actually live

The museum scene is extraordinary: the Gulbenkian (one of Europe’s underrated great museums), MAAT (contemporary art on the Tagus), the Azulejo museum

Excellent metro system

Trains Between Cities

- Portugal’s train system is clean, reliable, and affordable.
- Only book through the **official site**: [cp.pt](https://www.cp.pt/)

The hills and miradouros (viewpoints) reward walkers

Consider Lisbon as a shorter stay (4–5 days) rather than a full week base, perhaps combined with Porto.

## Coimbra
**Worth Considering**

Portugal’s great university city, often skipped by tourists.

One hour one minute to hour 10 minutes on the fast trains from Porto

One of the world’s oldest universities, with a stunning baroque library (the Joanina)
A real city with student energy and local life
	
Good food scene at local prices

On the main Lisbon–Porto rail line, making it perfect as a stop between the two

## Suggested Itinerary Shape

Given how well the Milano base + day trips model worked for you, I’d suggest:

Porto for 7–8 days as your main base, with day trips to Braga, Guimarães, and possibly Aveiro — then 3–4 days in Lisbon at the end before flying home.

This mirrors exactly what worked in Italy, uses Portugal’s excellent intercity trains, and keeps you in real urban environments the whole time.
