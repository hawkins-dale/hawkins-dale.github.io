### shit to do in Montpellier 
- **Explore the Écusson:** Get lost in the medieval center's narrow streets, shops, and cafes.
-  **Place de la Comédie:** The central hub, featuring the Three Graces Fountain and the Opera House.
- **[[Musée Fabre]]:** A premier fine arts museum.
- Contemporary art: [[MO.CO.]] and eat in its restaurant, Fawn
- **Beaches:** tram line 3 to the beach! Palavas-les-Flots or Carnon-Plage.  Or ride: [[Bikes in Montpellier]]
- **Centre Historique**. Take a look at the Salle Pétraque on the Place Pétraque. The medieval building and the others next to it are amazing.   

There are about 180 old mansions from the boom days (wine export and center of regional government)...followed by bust days when the vines died due to phylloxera.

- **Parc du Domaine de Méric:** A scenic park along the Lez River.
- **Pic Saint-Loup:** A popular hiking destination offering scenic wine tours in the surrounding hills

- **Arc de Triomphe & Promenade du Peyrou:** A historic arch and a scenic park offering panoramic views and sunset spots.
- TOUR DE LA BABOTE
- **Saint-Pierre Cathedral:** A dramatic Gothic cathedral.
-  **Visit a Winery:** Tour the nearby Languedoc vineyards.
- **Jardin des Plantes:** France's oldest botanical garden, established in 1593.
- **L’Arbre Blanc:** A modern, iconic apartment building with a *rooftop bar* for views.
- **Faculty of Medicine:** Visit one of the oldest in the world
