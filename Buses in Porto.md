


In general, the bus system in Porto works quite well. The schedules are usually met, the vehicles are safe and the best routes are in place to take you wherever you want to go.  
Moreover, there is free wifi in practically the entire bus fleet of STCP (and it works very well). Therefore, there is no reason to not take a bus in Porto!

## Andante Card

for bus and metro.  Buy and charge the [Andante Car](http://www.linhandante.com/ "Andante Card")[d](https://linhandante.com/) almost everywhere in the city. At the airport, metro and train stations, some bus stops, kiosks, Pay Shop agents (look for the red flags on newspaper shops, buildings or coffees) and many other places.
The card is 0.50€.  Bus rides are 1,20€.
You can pay with cash on the bus: 1,80€

Each bus is “codified” by a 3-digit sign and the first number is related with the destination place: 2 is for West Porto, 3 for North, 4 for East, 5 for Matosinhos, 6 for Maia, 7 for Valongo, 8 for Gondomar and 9 for Vila Nova de Gaia. In case of doubts, consult the bus stop itinerary, which indicates all the stops during your route.

- Respect the waiting line at the stop
- If needed, raise your arm when the bus you want to catch is coming. This way, the driver will know that you want to get inside
- Inspectors do come inside the bus asking for your (validated ) ticket
- If you’re not so certain about the limits (Zones) you’re traveling, ask for a map in a tourism station or consult the itinerary map at the stop
- You won’t be lost on the route. Inside the bus there’s a display with the name of the next stop and, in doubt, ask the driver – if he doesn’t understand your language, maybe someone will help you (lucky to have such friendly people!)
